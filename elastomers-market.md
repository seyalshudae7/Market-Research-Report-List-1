<p><strong>The Elastomers Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>SBR (Styrene Block Copolymers)</li><li>IIR (Butyl Elastomer)</li><li>NBR (Nitrile Elastomer)</li><li>ACM (Acrylic Elastomer)</li><li>EPM (Ethylene-Propylene Elastomer)</li><li>PEBA (Thermoplastic Polyether Block Amides)</li><li>SBC (Styrene Block Copolymers)</li><li>TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin)</li><li>TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethanes)</li><li>TPV (Thermoplastic Vulcanizates)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The elastomers market encompasses various types, each with unique properties and applications. SBR (Styrene Block Copolymers) and SBC (Styrene Block Copolymers) provide flexibility and high resilience, suitable for adhesives and coatings. IIR (Butyl Elastomer) is known for low gas permeability, ideal for inner tubes and sealants. NBR (Nitrile Elastomer) offers oil resistance, making it perfect for automotive seals. ACM (Acrylic Elastomer) withstands temperature extremes, while EPM (Ethylene-Propylene Elastomer) is valued for its rubber-like qualities. PEBA (Thermoplastic Polyether Block Amides) combines elasticity with chemical resistance. TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) and TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethanes) offer durability and flexibility, while TPV (Thermoplastic Vulcanizates) excels in heat resistance, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
