<p><ul><li>Ionic Emulsifier</li><li>Nonionic Emulsifier</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The synthetic emulsifier market is categorized into two main types: ionic and nonionic emulsifiers. Ionic emulsifiers are characterized by their charged molecules, which enhance stability in emulsions and are commonly used in food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals. Nonionic emulsifiers, on the other hand, contain neutral molecules, providing versatility and stability in various applications without the influence of charge. Both types play crucial roles in improving texture, consistency, and shelf life in diverse industries, catering to specific formulation needs.</p></p>
