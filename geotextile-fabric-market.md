<p><p>The geotextile fabric market is divided into two primary types: synthetic and natural. </p><p>Synthetic geotextiles are made from man-made fibers such as polyester, polypropylene, and polyethylene, offering durability, resistance to chemicals, and versatility for applications in drainage, erosion control, and road construction. </p><p>Natural geotextiles, on the other hand, are derived from organic materials like jute, coir, and bamboo. They are biodegradable and eco-friendly, making them suitable for soil reinforcement and environmental restoration projects. Both types serve critical roles in civil engineering and environmental management.</p></p>
